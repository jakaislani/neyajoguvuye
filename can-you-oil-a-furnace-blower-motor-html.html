Can you oil a furnace blower motor?

Cleaning and lubricating the furnace blower motor

Apply a few drops of oil to each of the oil ports to lubricate them. A few or three drops will be plenty. It is important not to use too much oil since excessive lubrication might lead to additional difficulties.

Also, what kind of oil is used in the blower motor of a furnace?

If the engine is equipped with oil ports, it should be greased once a year. To each port, add two or three drops of 10-weight nondetergent motor oil (not all-purpose oil) to dilute it to the desired concentration. Don’t lubricate your joints too much. Oil ports on the blower shaft should be lubricated on a yearly basis as well, using the same process as for the main shaft.

Second, is it possible to oil a fan motor?

 Make use of a high-quality oil that has been specifically created for high-speed motor shafts and bearings. It is not recommended to use oil that contains a detergent, such as automotive motor oil. Using detergent on the bearings of fan motors is not a good idea. Ensure that you have turned off the electricity to your system by turning off the disconnect switch or the A/C system breaker in the breaker box

In the same vein, how can I grease the blower motor in my furnace?

Don’t use too much lubricant. Some older furnaces have two motor bearings and two blower shaft bearings, both of which need to be lubricated once a year. Remove the oil caps after cleaning the area surrounding them. Replace the caps and apply two to three drops of lightweight machine oil to the threads. Don’t lubricate your joints too much!

Is it possible to put WD40 on the furnace motor?

A family member sprayed wd40 on the blower motor of an ancient fuel oil furnace. Wd40 is really more of a penetrating oil than a lubricant, thus it isn’t very effective. It’s a terrific product in its own right, but go for the Zoom spout or 3-in-1 type machine oil instead.
