An In-depth Look At The Lives of Novak Djokovic Children

Novak Djokovic children are lucky to have parents who cherish them and encourage them to pursue their dreams. 

Jelena Djokovic, Novak Djokovic’s wife, has been together with him for a long time. They started dating each other in 2005 and got engaged eight years later, in 2013. 

The pair tied the knot on July 10, 2014. Together they share two children. 

As parents, Novak and Jelena have faced the joys and challenges of raising their two children, Stefan and Tara. However, they still prioritize their roles as loving parents.

Novak and Jelena first met in high school, and their bond strengthened over time. 

They began dating formally in 2005, and together they experienced life’s highs and lows.

Throughout their relationship, they have supported one another, shared each other’s successes, and offered support when needed.

Presently, Novak Djokovic’s wife, Jelena, holds the position of National Director at the Novak Djokovic Foundation, which the pair established together.

Moreover, Jelena is also an active entrepreneur and humanitarian. 

Evaluating Djokovic’s Grand Slam record

Novak Djokovic’s 23rd grand slam victory has sparked discussions about his GOAT(greatest of all time) status. While some argue he deserves the title, others hesitate to endorse it fully.

They believe that players like Federer deserve equal recognition. However, it’s essential to question whether numbers alone define greatness.

Suppose an imaginary player who is 10 feet tall, serves aces consistently, and wins 30 grand slams. Would they be the GOAT? No, such a notion is ridiculous. 

Similarly, imagine a world war where a player named Dave wins 30 grand slams. 

Would Dave be the greatest? Not. Determining the GOAT involves more than statistics or circumstances. 

Djokovic’s achievements are remarkable, but the title of GOAT is subjective and complex.

Novak Djokovic children

Novak Djokovic and his wife, Jelena, are proud parents of two children. Their parenthood journey began with their son Stefan’s birth on October 22, 2014, just four months after their wedding. 

Novak Djokovic’s son, Stefan, has since followed in his father’s and his uncles’ footsteps, showing a huge interest in tennis. 

He is often seen with a racket, ready to practice and learn from his father’s expertise. 

The bond between Novak and Stefan increases beyond the tennis court, as they have enjoyed hitting sessions together, even during Novak’s victorious run at the 2022 Wimbledon tournament.

In September 2017, Novak and Jelena welcomed their second child, a daughter named Tara, into their lives. 

In contrast to her brother Stefan, Tara is not as interested in tennis as he is.

Instead, she is carving her path and exploring different attractions outside the sport.

Novak Djokovic children adore their father and affectionately call him “Tata,” which means Dad in Serbian. 

Stefan and Tara are often spotted alongside their mother whenever Novak competes, passionately cheering for their superstar dad from the sidelines.

Despite his success in tennis, Novak has made it clear that he does not want to force his children into the sport.

He values their freedom to choose their passions and interests. 

While Stefan is strongly willing towards tennis, Tara is on a different path, exploring her preferences outside of the sport. 

Novak Djokovic children have shared many heartfelt moments with him. 

He cherishes the times when Stefan demonstrates his forehands and backhands, engaging in playful “shadow tennis” with his father.
